Week outcomes

Knowledge outcomes

  • Understand company positioning, marketing goals, and KPIs.
  • Know each client’s business model, core offerings, and target segments.
  • Map each market: competitors, trends, regulations, seasonality, and channels.
  • Understand customer behavior drivers, barriers, and decision journey.

Deliverables by end of week

  • Client research (one per client).
  • Market landscape summary per client (competitors, differentiators, pricing, messaging themes).
  • Customer behavior notes (journey map and key insights).

Research standards

  • Evidence first: Prefer primary sources (client materials, analytics, CRM, surveys, support tickets, sales notes) over opinions.
  • Document assumptions: Clearly label what is confirmed vs. inferred.
  • Cross-check: Validate claims with at least two sources when possible.
  • Reusable outputs: Write research as if others will use it next month.

Day-by-day plan

Day 1: Orientation and context Focus: systems, people, and a clear picture of success
  • Company overview: mission, value proposition, brand guidelines, tone of voice, and key messages.
  • Meet manager (45–60 min): priorities, what “good” looks like, immediate expectations.
  • Meet onboarding buddy (30 min): workflows, tools, and how requests typically come in.
  • Access setup: analytics platforms, ad accounts, CRM, project tools, shared drive, reporting dashboards.
  • Review current marketing plan: goals, active campaigns, quarterly themes, key KPIs.
  • Review client list: services/products, contract scope, current status, major stakeholders.

Day 1 outputs

  • Personal checklist completed: tools access and logins verified.
  • One-page summary: team structure, KPIs, and current campaign snapshot.
  • Questions list: gaps you need answered to research clients correctly.
Day 2: Client research foundations Focus: understand each client’s business and objectives
  • For each client, collect core materials: pitch decks, past reports, creative briefs, content calendars, performance summaries.
  • Clarify objectives: acquisition, retention, awareness, lead quality, revenue, engagement, or other.
  • Review brand constraints: compliance, approvals, do’s and don’ts, and typical turnaround times.
  • Interview internal stakeholders (short calls or async notes): account lead, designer, media buyer, or content lead.
  • Create a “client facts” section: product lines, pricing approach, key regions, key audiences, key channels used.

Day 2 outputs

  • Draft client research one-pager template filled for at least 50% of clients.
  • Stakeholder notes: objectives, constraints, current pain points.
Day 3: Market landscape and competition Focus: map the market so decisions have context
  • Identify top competitors per client (direct and indirect) and substitute solutions.
  • Compare positioning: differentiators, messaging themes, value props, proof points.
  • Channel presence scan: SEO topics, paid ads angles, social platforms, partnerships, marketplaces.
  • Pricing and packaging (where visible): plans, offers, bundles, free trials, guarantees.
  • Market drivers: seasonality, regulations, macro trends, and buyer constraints.

Day 3 outputs

  • Market landscape summary per client (competitor table plus key takeaways).
  • Short list of 3–5 opportunities and 3–5 risks per client.
Day 4: Customer behavior and decision journey Focus: why customers act, not just what they do
  • Review customer data: personas (if any), surveys, user interviews, reviews, support tickets, sales call notes.
  • Analyze funnel behavior: top entry points, conversion drop-offs, time-to-convert, repeat purchase patterns.
  • Map the customer journey:
    • Triggers and motivations
    • Information sources and trust drivers
    • Objections, friction, and anxiety points
    • Moments that cause comparison shopping
    • Reasons for churn or inactivity
  • Identify “behavioral levers”: urgency, social proof, clarity of value, risk reversal, onboarding, habit formation.

Day 4 outputs

  • Customer behavior notes per client: motivations, barriers, and top 3 decision factors.
  • One journey map per client (simple stages with key insights and proposed tests).
Day 5: Synthesis, presentation, and next steps Focus: turn research into priorities and actions
  • Synthesize findings into a clear narrative for each client: what matters most and why.
  • Define initial hypotheses and tests:
    • Channel hypothesis: where to focus next and what to stop doing
    • Message hypothesis: what promise resonates and what proof is needed
    • Offer hypothesis: what to change about pricing, packaging, or incentive
  • Create a 30/60/90-day plan: learning goals, quick wins, experiments, and reporting cadence.
  • Present to manager and key stakeholders (20–30 min) with a short Q&A.

Day 5 outputs

  • Final one-pagers and market summaries stored in the shared drive.
  • Proposed quick wins list (prioritized by impact, confidence, and effort).
  • 30/60/90-day plan drafted and aligned with manager.

Client research one-pager template

Copy this section for each client. Keep it short, factual, and sourced.

Section What to capture
Client overview Industry, offerings, regions served, business model, key stakeholders, scope of work
Objectives and KPIs Primary goals, target KPIs, constraints, reporting cadence
Target customers Segments, jobs-to-be-done, primary use cases, high-value cohorts
Customer behavior Triggers, motivations, objections, decision factors, journey stages, conversion friction
Market landscape Main competitors, differentiators, pricing cues, messaging themes, channel presence
Channel performance snapshot What is working, what is not, top content/ads, funnel issues, tracking gaps
Opportunities and risks 3–5 actionable opportunities; 3–5 risks; what data is missing
First tests 2–4 experiment ideas with hypothesis, success metric, and effort estimate

Manager check-in agenda (use on Day 1 and Day 5)

  • Status: what you completed since last check-in.
  • Insights: the most important finding and why it matters.
  • Risks and blockers: access, data gaps, unclear priorities.
  • Next steps: experiments and deliverables for the coming week.

Note

If research reveals unclear positioning or conflicting objectives, escalate early with concrete examples and recommended options.
