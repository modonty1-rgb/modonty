# Marketing Analytics Brief — Modonty

**To:** Marketing team  
**From:** Senior Marketing Manager  
**Purpose:** What we track today, what you can measure, and how to use it for campaigns and optimization.

---

## 1. Why this matters

We now have **end-to-end visibility** on the Modonty site: we know how people find us, what they view, what they click, how long they stay, and when they convert. This brief summarizes what’s available so you can plan campaigns, report performance, and optimize based on real data.

---

## 2. What we track (in plain terms)

**Article and client page views.** We count every time someone opens an article or a client profile. For you: reach and interest — which content and which clients get traffic.
**Likes, dislikes, favorites.** We record when someone likes, dislikes, or favorites an article. For you: sentiment and intent — which articles resonate.

**Shares.** We record when someone shares an article (e.g. to social or copy link). For you: viral potential — which content gets shared and where.

**Comments and replies.** We record when someone posts a comment or reply, and when they like or dislike a comment. For you: community and depth of engagement.

**Newsletter signups (client and global).** We record every subscription to a client's newsletter and to the global news newsletter. For you: list growth and lead capture by source.

**Contact form submissions.** We record every message sent via the contact form. For you: inquiries and lead quality.

**Follows (client profiles).** We record when someone follows a client. For you: brand affinity and audience building.

**User registrations.** We record every new account signup. For you: signups and funnel.
**CTA clicks (8 tracked CTAs).** Which actions drive clicks: “Read more”, “Subscribe”, “Visit client”, “Ask client”, “Comment”, “Share”, “More/Related articles”, “Visit website”. Use this to compare CTA performance and optimize placement.
**In-article link clicks.** We record when someone clicks any link inside the article body (internal or external). For you: which links get clicked — use for content and partnership reporting.
**Conversions.** Every contact submit, signup, and newsletter signup is recorded as a conversion. For you: conversion rate and conversion type so you can measure funnel and ROI.
**Session engagement.** For each article view we store time on page, scroll depth, and whether they bounced (e.g. left in under 30 seconds). For you: content quality and layout — see what keeps people reading.
**Traffic source and referrer.** We store where the visit came from (e.g. organic, referral). For you: attribution and channel mix.
**Lead scoring (HOT / WARM / COLD).** In the client console, leads are scored by engagement. Use “Refresh scores” to update; then use the Leads page for prioritization and outreach.

All of the above are **live**: when a visitor does the action on the site, we record it. The client console reads this data for dashboards and reports.

---

## 3. What you can measure and report

- **Reach:** Article and client profile views over time; top content and top clients.
- **Engagement:** Time on page, scroll depth, bounce rate, likes, comments, shares, follows.
- **Funnel:** Views → CTA clicks → conversions (contact, signup, newsletter). You can report conversion rate and drop-off by step.
- **Attribution:** Traffic source and referrer per view/session so you can attribute conversions to channel.
- **CTA performance:** Which of the 8 CTAs get the most clicks; optimize placement and copy based on data.
- **Content performance:** Which articles and in-article links get clicks; use for content strategy and partnerships.
- **Lead quality:** In the console, use the Leads page (after “Refresh scores”) to see HOT/WARM/COLD and prioritize outreach.

---

## 4. Where you see the data

- **Client console:** Dashboards, analytics, campaigns, comments, subscribers, leads, etc. All fed by the data above.
- **Leads page:** Run “Refresh scores” when you want updated lead scores; then use the table and filters for prioritization.

For technical details (when each event fires, how to test, what’s deferred), see **Important_ANALYTICS_DATA_SOURCES_REPORT.md**.

---

## 5. What’s not in place yet (and when we’ll add it)

- **Campaign/UTM tracking:** We are **not** yet recording UTM parameters (e.g. from paid or email links). When we launch campaigns with UTM-tagged URLs, we’ll turn this on so you can report by campaign and ad set.
- **Cost/impressions/clicks from ad platforms:** These will be added via sync or manual input later, not from the site itself.

Until then, you can still use traffic source and referrer (current Analytics) for basic channel attribution.

---

## 6. Quick action checklist for marketing

- Use the **console** for weekly/monthly reports: views, engagement, conversions, and lead counts.
- Use **CTA click** data to compare the 8 CTAs and improve placement and copy.
- Use **conversion** data to report conversion rate and to attribute contact/signup/newsletter to campaigns (via source/referrer).
- Use the **Leads** page in the console (after “Refresh scores”) to prioritize HOT/WARM leads for sales or nurture.
- When we launch UTM-based campaigns, we’ll confirm once campaign tracking is on so you can report by campaign and medium.

---

## 7. One-pager summary

We track: **views** (article + client), **engagement** (likes, comments, shares, follows, time on page, scroll, bounce), **8 CTA clicks**, **in-article link clicks**, **conversions** (contact, signup, newsletter), **traffic source/referrer**, and **lead scores** (HOT/WARM/COLD in the console).  

You can: **report reach and engagement**, **measure funnel and conversion rate**, **attribute by source**, **optimize CTAs and content**, and **prioritize leads**.  

Not yet: **UTM/campaign tracking** (coming when we use campaign links); **ad cost/impressions** (later via sync or manual).
